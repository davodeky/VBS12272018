Last Sunday's Sermon

Luke 1:67-79

  • Zacharias served God faithfully despite having not had a chance to have children.
  • It is one thing to serve God expecting something, but it is another thing serving Him not expecting something.
  • We should not serve God because we need, but because He is God!
  • Zacharias knew how to serve God in integrity, despite the challenge he had.
  • Zacharias encountered the angel of the Lord while he was serving God.
  • There is a place where a spectacular miracle will find you.
  • There are miracles that can happen anywhere.
  • Spectacular miracles, however, find you in specific places, and most of the times it is in the presence of the Lord.
  • Zacharias could have been discouraged, but he stayed steadfast.
  • Zacharias could have already stopped to praying for a child- given his age.
  • When the angel visited Zacharias to tell him that his prayer had been answered, that could have been a prayer of far back when he was a youth.
  • It does not matter how long you have waited, if you have prayed and believed, God will always fulfil His promise.
  • There is nothing impossible to our God.
  • If God has promised you, even if the world says it is too late, it is never too late on God's calender!
  • God does not operate like Men do.
  • To God, it never too late.
  • Zacharias expressed doubt about what God was about to do in His life.
  • Zacharias should certainly have known the testimony of Abraham, but why did he doubt?
  • The devil has a tendency of blinding us from what God has done is capable of doing in our lives.
  • God does mighty and amazing things in our lives everyday, but we still have doubt.
  • We have all seen enough miracle to know that God is able to sustain us to the end.
  • Some of us are still standing by the grace and the grace of God alone!
  • Zacharias was made mute because he doubted the promise of God.
  • The times when the breakthrough is very close is when everything seems worse and impossible.
  • Zacharias went in as a fatherless guy, but he walked out with a miracle.
  • You cannot go into the presence of the Lord and come out empty-handed.
  • In verse 68, when Zacharias sang "“Blessed is the Lord God of Israel, For He has visited and redeemed His people," he meant God has remembered.
  • Zacharias means the Lord remembers- so he was carrying his miracle all along, he just didn't know whout it.
  • Sometimes we wait for God to do some things, yet those things are already in our presence.
  • We need to start celebrating all gifts that God has given unto us.
  • Some of us doubt too much!
  • Everything on this earth has an expiry date, but the promises of God do not expire- the promises that He made to Abraham lived on through Isaac and Jacob and so did the promise that He made to Zacharias.
  • In verse 76, Zacharias speaks to his miracle- he wasn't out celebrating.
  • We sometimes take our miracles and run, and we wonder why the miracles make us cry.
  • Zacharias prophesized to his miracle- baby John.
  • If God has blessed you- be you a parent, a spouse, a worker, a student- you should go and look at your miracle and speak to it.
  • Why would God bless you, if you don't have a song?
  • Why would God bless you if you don't have a song of thanksgiving?
